PHI CHAPTER :: UNIVERSITY OF FLORIDA

Chapter Name:The Fortress of Phire

Founding Date: March 5, 1995

Phi Chapter was the first chapter of the fraternity outside the tri-state area. Aways leading the way, Phi Chapter was instrumental in the development of Lambda Theta Phi and the Latino Greeks in general. Its nickname was coined by Founding Brothers Eric Lopez and Ricardo Urrusuno. Being that they were establishing the first chapter in the south, they believed the Lambdas of Phi were destined to become a new genesis for Conquistadores to be born all about Florida. The intention of the founding brothers was to erect a fraternal fortress that would be “solid as stone” growing large enough to one day touch the flaming sun so characteristic of their state, and thus becoming THE FORTRESS OF PHIRE.

Since it’s founding, the brothers of Phi Chapter strove for excellence not only with the Fraternity but on the University of Florida campus. In 1999 we helped found the Multicultural Greek Council. Leading the other three founding organizations, Phi Chapter lead in the construction of the foundation of a council that now houses 13 organizations. Phi Chapter also introduced benchmark programming with the creation of Salsamania in 2001. The event became very large and successful so the University took it over as a welcome event for incoming freshmen and named it Gatormania, the largest Summer event at The University of Florida.

Among these things, Phi Chapter helped create the Hispanic Latino Collegiate Forum at UF and multiple other events that have become traditional events for Hispanic Culture on the University of Florida campus. Phi Chapter has been awarded Sector Chapter of the Year for two consecutive years (2012-2013), Undergraduate National Chapter of the Year (2007), and highest GPA in the Multicultural Greek Council (2012-2013) and second highest GPA among all Greek chapters on campus.
