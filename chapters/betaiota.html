BETA IOTA CHAPTER :: FLORIDA STATE UNIVERSITY

Chapter Name: The Warriors of the Tribal Lands

Founding Date: October 29, 2000

In 2000, the Latino population had virtually no representation at the campus of Florida State University. Because of this, five men embarked on a journey to bring Lambda Theta Phi Latin Fraternity Incorporated to this campus. Since coming on to this campus, the Beta Iota Chapter has set an example for all other multicultural greeks. In their formative years, the Beta Iota Chapter has been responsible for leading groups to create lasting change on FSUâ€™s campus. From bringing Pollo Tropical on campus for Latin students to helping spearhead student protests for higher tuition that succeeding in keeping FSU the most cost effective education, the Beta Iota Chapter has continually strived from its inception to empower others.

Even now, the Beta Iota Chapter maintains this spirit of empowerment. Recently, the Brothers helped the Dream Defenders in their protest for a special session of the Florida Legislature. Along with empowering others, the Beta Iota Chapter strives to lead by example. As of this last Spring, the Beta Iota Chapter has the highest Grade Point Average in the Multicultural Greek Council and the second highest overall. Furthermore, the Beta Iota Chapter has produced the first, and only, Hispanic/Latino Student Senate President to this very day.
